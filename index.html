In 2010, I graduated high school. I didn’t know how to write an essay. I knew nothing of finances. I couldn’t perform math beyond division. What I knew was work -hard, dirty, never-ending work. 60+ hours a week in my father’s lawn maintenance business taught me that.

It was time for a change.

I then attended a school that crammed 90 credits into 18 months. I left with a wife and a mind equipped for critical thinking -and debt. So I worked and studied independently and worked some more. The next 3 years became a blur of various jobs and online schooling. When I ended, I had a bachelor’s degree, experience with multiple entry-level jobs, but no purpose.

It was time for a change.

My wife and I moved to Colorado -pioneers headed West for opportunity. We had little prospects, but we did what we knew best: worked hard. Gradually, we climbed the ladder of success through various jobs in different sectors, with our last one being the financial sector, talking to people about retirement options, and financial stability, and blah, blah, blah (insert Khafka story here). This phase of my life gave me two investor licenses and a bad habit of trying to forget my job when I wasn’t there.

It was time for a change.

Since then, my wife and I have dropped everything to become front-end web designers. We want to spend our lives talking about color palettes, the effect of shapes on people’s perceptions of band identity, and how to make websites interactive. We’ve thrown ourselves into unknown territory repeatedly for the sake of breaking ground in the energetic, vibrant, and curious field of design.

Why did both my wife and I make the jump into a new career path together? Good question! We did so because we were both interested in the field, and we didn’t want separate career paths to act as a barrier to each other's lives. If I’m frustrated with a design issue, I want to be able to have an intelligible conversation with her about it, and vice versa. We don’t want an unbridgeable gap between us.

That’s one thing I don’t plan to change :)
