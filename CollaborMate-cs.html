CollaborMate

2016 - UX/UI Designer & Front-End Developer

Introduction

From the onset, this project had two goals -one personal and one practical. My personal goal was to dissect and understand the design process from beginning to end to be able to conceptualize and present a materialized product. The practical aim of the project was to create a platform for multiple users to share multiple types of media in order to collaborate on a project. CollaborMate set out to help people on a project but CollaborMate helped me learn how to develop a project.

Problem

My design team and I were brought on board to become acquainted with project development by creating a stylish simple way for teams to collaborate on projects in a digital space. The goal was to the learn the ins and outs of design and working as a team to accomplish a goal while also building a space for others to do the same. In a world becoming more and more digital, it’s increasingly difficult to work as a team on projects if you don’t have a digital space to store all your media files and share them so they can be accessed from anywhere, anytime.

Solution

Our solution was to conduct a SWOT analysis of our competitors (Google Drive, Evernote, SimpleNote) and then figure out a branding strategy that would appeal to our users. We conducted surveys to discover who our primary users were and what was most important to them. With our limited experience our solution was to listen to the instrusty experts, websites that are working, and our users to figure out how to structure the website. We then conducted several interviews and created personas to help guide us in our design process.

Brand Identity

Logo

With our audience defined, we came up with a lot of intricate and detailed logo designs but quickly learned it was too taxing on the eyes because of complicated visuals. This weakened our brand identity. Additionally, we needed to recognize the importance of keeping our logo simple for the sake of scalability. Logos need to fit on multiple surfaces so they have to be able to scale down and up with ease. Because of this we needed to eliminate all the colors too because black and white is the most basic way to view a logo on multiple surfaces, especially if those surfaces are colored.

Color Palette

With a user-first model we needed to get into the mind of our users to see how colors communicated meaning to them. Knowing this information would help us to choose a color palette that would allow our users to feel comfortable using our site. We started with bright colors but quickly discovered they didn’t provide us with the professional feeling we were going for so we ended up with the darker blues.

User Flows & Stories

Keeping one eye on our audience, it was time to develop the site with them in mind. Our methodology was to begin with user stories to define the expectations of our users, followed by diagrams of the site and pages the user would find expected info. With this blueprint defined, we then developed low fidelity mockups to begin the visualization of each page. Once we were satisfied with the layout, we tested and adjusted the layout against user feedback. Finally, we developed high fidelity mockups of the site.

Final Thoughts

What worked? We designed a functional static site and achieved the goal of developing a project from start to finish. UX research helped us improve our mockups and branding for each iteration.

What didn't work? We learned that sticking to simple guidelines produced a generic-looking website that wasn’t as compelling as we would have liked it.

Any surprises? User feedback is more valuable that we anticipated. It identified not only areas of opportunity but also areas of our natural talent.

Learning lessons: We went from being unfamiliar with both the programs and processes of design to becoming acquainted with programs such as AI, PS, and Sketch. We learned about hierarchy, color palettes, typography, personas, user stories and all the other wonderful aspects that come into play in UX/UI and frontend development. This project provided a platform for us to increase our expertise.
