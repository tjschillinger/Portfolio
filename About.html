My Archimedean Point

My career in UX design stems from my relentless will to answer the question, what is the good life? This question has led me to thumb through the minds of philosophers, uproot myself from my home state, and walk up to strangers at gas stations, asking have seen the promotion today? The accumulation of my pursuit has led me to UX design, because chipping away at the answer of what is the good life is the essence of good UX design. The good life is a story, so is good UX. The good life is social, so is good UX. The good life takes place in an established environment, so does good UX.

My passion to understand the good life informs and reinforces how I design. It is me putting to practicing what I’ve learned along my journey. I hear Nietzsche ranting about the will to power when I gamifying simple tasks. I keep Dante’s idea of slothfulness in mind when I wireframe. I listen to Occam sharpen his razor when I think about the scope of project features. All these things and more contribute to my process when I design because if users can’t add a little good to their lives when they use my design, then they won’t use it.

Moving Forward

Through my experiences I have learned my life was only good when I have improved the lives of others. That is, I didn't achieve the good life by grabbing it for myself but by giving it to others. And isn’t that the core of good UX/UI? Don’t let your prejudices get in the way, conduct user research. Don’t let your narrative get in the way, build user stories. Don’t let your pride plug your ears, send out user testing.

The world is always changing and technology along with it, but people will always need someone to listen and provide thoughtful solutions. Providing those solutions, for me, is living the good life.
